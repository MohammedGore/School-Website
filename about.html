Our history

The history of computer science society can be traced back to the early 20th century when the first electronic computers were developed. During this time, computer science as a field was primarily focused on the development and improvement of these machines.

In the 1940s and 1950s, computer science began to evolve as a distinct discipline with the introduction of programming languages and the development of software. The creation of the first high-level programming language, Fortran, in 1957, marked a significant milestone in the field.

Our Vision

The vision of computer science is to continuously push the boundaries of technology and innovation. It aims to solve complex problems, improve efficiency, and enhance the quality of life for individuals and society as a whole.

Our Mission

The mission of computer science is to advance knowledge and understanding of computing systems and their applications. It involves designing, developing, and analyzing algorithms, software, and hardware to solve complex problems and improve efficiency.

What We Do

Computer science encourages creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. It empowers individuals to become innovators and creators in the digital age. By studying computer science, you can unlock endless opportunities and contribute to shaping the future of technology.
